Engineer Open-Sources Voice-to-Text Desktop App

By Ibrahimsha

Open Source Correspondent

In a move described by local developers as "about time," the engineer has released Voxly — a minimalist voice-to-text desktop application — to the open-source community under public license.

The application, built atop an unusual Tauri, Rust, and SolidJS stack, allows users to hold a hotkey, speak naturally, and have their words transcribed and auto-pasted into any application. Cloud transcription is handled via OpenAI Whisper or Groq.

"I got tired of typing," the engineer stated. Sources confirm this was not, in fact, a joke.

The Man Behind the Pipeline: A Profile

Ibrahimsha is a backend and AI systems engineer based in southern India, building remotely for Singapore startups. With 7+ years of experience — from Education ERPs and platforms serving 2 million users to production RAG pipelines processing 100+ concurrent LLM calls — he has built a career on the principle that good infrastructure should be invisible.

His early career at Anubavam Technologies and KnackForge saw him shipping chatbots, email automation systems, and full-stack platforms across six concurrent projects. "Those years taught me that deadlines are real and sleep is optional," he recalls.

"When systems run right, they disappear into the product."

Today, as Lead Engineer at 01Digital, he architects multi-agent AI systems, mentors junior engineers to mid-level, and hires technical talent through custom assessments he designed himself.

When not deploying to Fargate at unusual hours, he is believed to be contributing to open-source projects and consuming unreasonable quantities of filter coffee.
